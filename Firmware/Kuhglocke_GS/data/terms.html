Ground Station Radio Terminology

A small overview of common phrases and acronyms surrounding the ground station and general radio operation.

RSSI = Received Signal Strength Indicator. Always negative. Higher value is better.

SNR = Signal to Noise Ratio. Usually positive, can be negative. Higher value better. Our range is approximately 16dB (best) to -15dB (worst).

Frequency Error = Offset between receiver centre frequency and incoming LoRa signal. Calculated as RecvFreq-TransFreq, closer to zero is better. If greater than 25% of bandwidth, may not capture signal.

Frequency is the carrier frequency for data. Higher frequency means shorter range but faster transmission.

Bandwidth is the frequency used to encode data within the carrier. Higher frequency means shorter range but faster transmission.

Spreading Factor is how much a transmission is spread out. Higher spreading means higher range, but slower transmission.

Coding Rate is how many bits are transmitted to yield 4 useful bits. Higher coding rate is more reliable, but slower. Receiver does not need to know this.

Frequency Correction is an offset applied to the receiver's frequency to correct the frequency error from transmitter and receiver oscillator tolerances. Frequency shifts with temperature, so this is not a perfect solution.

AFC = Automatic Frequency Correction/Control. The term AFC is used to describe a circuit that adjusts itself to stay in resonance with an external frequency. For Kuhglocke, this is a software algorithm which attempts to reduce the frequency error between rocket and ground station due to thermal drift.

TCXO = Temperature Compensated Crystal Oscillator. A type of precision clock that remains stable over a wide range of temperatures. Using a radio transceiver based on an TCXO would signigicantly reduce frequency error due to thermal drift.
